Spiritual

In light of the surfeit of 'logic' floating around

2016-07-26

Shivakumar D


The omnipotent and infinite lord, is formless. He does not have any form………… Isn’t there an inherent contradiction in that?

The Lord is omnipotent generally, but is impotent to assume a form? And the Lord, whose limits we fix in finite terms, is infinite?

The moment we limit the possibilities of what the Lord can or cannot, we reduce the Lord to finite and definite. Conversely, how can one fathom infinity to go so far as to constrain it?

Either emphatic assertion, that he is only with form or the one that precludes form are both logically limiting. As Sri Ramakrishna used to say, when in the gaseous state, H2O has no form; in the liquid state, it is free flowing and in the solid state, it simply takes the shape of the container.

Let’s make an allowance for both, but what is the point worshipping a stone or wooden image? Does idol worship ever make sense? Well, the idol itself is never the target or object of worship.

In course of his travels, Swami Vivekananda reached Alwar and was accosted by the king Mangal Singh. The Maharaja asked him, “I find the image-worship you people engage in meaningless. Why would anyone want to worship clay, metal or stone?” Swami Vivekananda looked around and saw a painting of the king’s father hanging on the wall, and requested that it be brought down to him. He then asked the king to spit on it. The king was infuriated and burst out “How can I spit on my father? What guts should you have to seek it!” Swami Vivekananda exclaimed, “But where is your father here? Isn’t it paint and paper?” The king was tongue tied, it was his own logic after all. Swami Vivekananda then went on to explain that the idol is simply meant to remind the worshipper of the principle it symbolized or represented. All worship offered, was to the Being it reminded of and not itself.

But why leave room for form and formless, and on top of it multiple forms? A “one-size-fits-all” approach wouldn’t really benefit everybody. If the only shop in town that sold clothes, had only one size? And only one pattern and colour? Would it suit everybody? The contemplation, comprehension and assimilation levels vary between people, just as physiques do. He chooses that form that is conducive to his state.

Doesn’t it mean there are many Gods that get worshipped? There are cookies shaped as a key, a bird, a house etc. Children fight over them, with one choosing a house and another a key. Does it mean that each child is eating many different biscuits? Forms vary, but the fundamental substance remains the same. To the child, a particular form beckons. So also, to the worshipper. All rivers finally culminate in the ocean.
